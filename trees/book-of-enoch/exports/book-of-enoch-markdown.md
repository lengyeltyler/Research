## Book of Enoch

- Type: root
- Status: researching
- Tags: book-of-enoch, second-temple, apocrypha, pseudepigrapha, ancient-literature

### Summary
The Book of Enoch is a collection of ancient Jewish apocalyptic writings associated with Enoch, the seventh generation from Adam. It is especially important for understanding Second Temple Jewish ideas about angels, watchers, judgment, heavenly journeys, messianic expectation, and the background of some later Christian and Islamic traditions.

### Notes
Use this root as the central research ledger for tracing the Book of Enoch's sections, themes, textual history, religious influence, and unresolved historical questions.

### Claims
- 1 Enoch is not one single uniform book but a composite collection of several writings.
- The Book of the Watchers is one of the oldest and most influential sections of 1 Enoch.
- Enochic literature shaped later Jewish and Christian apocalyptic imagination.

### Open Questions
- How should the different sections of 1 Enoch be dated?
- Why was 1 Enoch preserved as scripture in the Ethiopian Orthodox tradition but not most Jewish or Christian canons?
- How did Enochic ideas influence later apocalyptic and early Christian writings?
- What relationship exists between the Watchers tradition, Genesis 6, and later demonology?
