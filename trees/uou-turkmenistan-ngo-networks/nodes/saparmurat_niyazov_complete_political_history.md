# Saparmurat Niyazov complete political history

## Summary
Niyazov's life, Soviet rise, post-Soviet consolidation, Turkmenbashi personality cult, censorship, and NGO suppression context.

## Notes
Niyazov was born in 1940. His father died in World War II, and the 1948 Ashgabat earthquake killed his mother and siblings. He grew up in a Soviet orphanage, studied engineering, rose through the Communist Party, and was elevated under Gorbachev. Independence did not begin as a competitive democratic break: the October 1991 referendum concerned independence, while Niyazov was already the Soviet-era leader and later consolidated power through an uncontested 1992 election and authoritarian presidential rule. His Turkmenbashi personality cult, censorship, Ruhnama ideology, and post-2002 assassination-attempt crackdown form the necessary context for NGO vulnerability.
